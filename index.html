3D Printing at Maryville College

I have been working with 3D printers since 2020. This work as led to a student organization on 3D printing, a course on the topic, a Makerspace at MC, and more.

In the Spring of 2021, we offered a special topis on class on 3D printing: MTH 149 Introduction to 3D printing. This course is a direct application of geometric and analytical thinking which teaches students computer-aided design (CAD) through applications like Tinkercad and Fusion 360. Students will apply geometric reasoning to design, draft, 3D print, and iterate models to solve real life problems. Students will learn about the process of Fused Deposition Modeling and Sterolithgraphy in this course. The course also includes an introduction to the hardware, firmware, and software involved in 3D printing along with how mathematics is applied in these.

A little bit about myself (Dr. S).

Very often in mathematics we are asked and taught how to find x. Since my youth, regardless of the mathematical algorithm being taught (from polynomial long division to calculations of cosets) I have always been more interested in the Y (why). Y does this algorithm work in the cases it does, Y does it fail in certain situations, Y do we need or learn this method... etc. Just as important (or arguably more so) than the search for x is finding why. In the realm of abstract mathematics this often takes on the form of Y is it true. In the realm of the applied, it lends to the importance of when a method can be used and can help a user possibly find new uses for the method or maybe even new methods.

I am from East Tennessee, growing up in West Knoxville. I attended Karns Elementary, Middle, and High School. I spent an amazing four years at Maryville College (MC) earning a B.A. in both Mathematics and Computer Science. I then attend graduate school at UTK where I learned a lot about Abstract Algebra from my adviser Dr. David Anderson. I have been a faculty member of MC since the 2014-2015 academic year
